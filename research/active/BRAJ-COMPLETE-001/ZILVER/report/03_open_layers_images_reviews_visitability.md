# 03 — Images, reviews, visitability and participation

## Ten-function image dossiers

No candidate reaches ten independently attributable, functionally distinct and sufficiently current images. Official/government pages provide usable physical-identity and promotional visual leads for several major temples, but do not cover all required functions: normal entrance, sacred centre, devotees, sitting, ordinary crowd, festival peak, surroundings/commercial pressure, maintenance/water condition and daypart atmosphere. `images.jsonl` preserves one audited shortfall record for every candidate and never counts duplicated thumbnails or unattributed social reposts.

The strongest partial visual bases are Banke Bihari, ISKCON Krishna-Balaram, Radha Rani Temple, Kirti Mandir and Kusum Sarovar. The weakest are the distributed water objects, small lila-places, Ter Kadamba, Prem Sarovar, Pavana Sarovar and the conflicted Gokul record.

## Reviews and atmosphere

A systematic recent multi-platform sample could not be assembled for every candidate. Reliable recurring signals are therefore limited to thematic, not statistical, conclusions:

- Banke Bihari: absorbing/intense darshan alongside crowd, narrow-lane and access-control pressure.
- ISKCON Vrindavan: organized international setting, kirtan and clearer participation structure, with institutional busyness.
- Nidhivan: sacred/mystical grove experience combined with tourist pressure and strict night boundary.
- Janmabhoomi: intense worship and security friction.
- Vishram Ghat and kund/sarovar objects: strong devotional atmosphere with recurring water, cleanliness, commerce and crowd questions.
- Govardhan Parikrama: immersive pilgrimage with heat, physical strain, traffic and festival differences.
- Radha Rani/Nand Bhavan: hilltop devotion and views balanced against stairs and festival pressure.
- Kirti Mandir: polished modern architecture, organized devotion and seva; different atmosphere from old Braj temples.

These themes remain `EXPERIENTIALLY_REPORTED` or `OPEN`, never historical proof.

## Current visitability and etiquette

Complete July 2026 regimes are not established for any candidate. The technically correct status remains `PRACTICAL_CHECK_REQUIRED` wherever direct official current rules are absent.

Common respectful baseline, not a substitute for direct confirmation: modest clothing, shoes removed where required, no photography of sanctums or devotees without permission, avoid obstructing darshan, follow separate queues and crowd-control instructions, do not enter or bathe in sacred water without local confirmation, and ask before offering food, milk or flowers. ISKCON has the strongest institutional basis for foreign/non-initiated participation. Small lineage ashrams and resident bhajan places require more direct permission than public temples.

Banke Bihari is specifically volatile because court-supervised management and queue reforms remain active. Janmabhoomi is specifically volatile because security measures and prohibited items may change. Nidhivan's night closure is a hard tradition/access boundary requiring current on-site compliance.
